How many Ukrainians are there in the U.S.?

There are at least 1,000,000 people in the U.S. that either have Ukrainian ancestry or are Ukrainian-born themselves1. This is more than 4x times more than in Germany.

Is that a lot?

Imagine, if just 1 out of 100 of all the Ukrainians in the U.S. attends this meeting, that would be 10,000 people — enough to get the necessary attention to the issue.

How Will This Gathering Help Ukraine?

Short answer:

It will help Ukraine get the heavy military support, necessary for stopping the war.

Long answer:

The russian army outnumbers the Ukrainian army in terms of military vehicles considerably

Ukraine has received mostly light military equipment. It is great for defending but not enough to counterattack. Ukraine needs heavy weapons to win the war and drive the occupants back

Military experts and analysts have made a list of necessary weapons. The list includes heavy artillery, tanks, armored vehicles, military trucks, and fighter jets; most of them are Soviet-made so that Ukrainian soldiers already know how to operate them

Is It Safe for Me to Protest?

Yes, it is safe for you. If you have any concerns relating to losing your job or getting deported because of participating in a protest in the United States, you should know that you have nothing to fear as long as you don’t break the law.

Everyone living in the United States, including immigrants, has certain constitutional rights that allow them to protest.

You will not be under the risk of deportation unless you assault or aggravate a police officer, disrupt public order, or obstruct justice.

You will not be under the risk of getting fired as long as you protest during non-work hours.

You can read more about your rights to protest here: Know Your Rights as An Immigrant In USA Before Attending Any Peaceful Protest.
